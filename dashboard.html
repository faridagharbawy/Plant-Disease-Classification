Why ResNet50 for PlantVillage?
Residual (skip) connections address the vanishing gradient problem in very deep networks, enabling learning of subtle disease texture differences between visually similar classes (e.g., early vs. late blight). The rich 2048-D feature vector via GlobalAveragePooling provides exceptional discriminative power for the SVM classifier. ResNet50 is widely cited in agricultural AI for leaf disease classification tasks (Mohanty et al., 2016, Front. Plant Sci.; Ferentinos, 2018, Computers and Electronics in Agriculture).

Bottleneck design (He et al., 2016, Fig. 5): each block uses 1×1 convolutions to reduce/restore channel dimensions around a 3×3 conv. Identity shortcuts added element-wise. Projection shortcuts (1×1 conv) used when dimensions change. This design enables stable training of 50+ layer networks.

4.2 — Conclusions

i. Performance: ML vs End-to-End DL

The End-to-End ResNet50 (Approach 2) outperforms the ResNet50+SVM hybrid (Approach 1) across all metrics. While the SVM achieves competitive results leveraging frozen ImageNet features, the E2E model's task-specific backpropagation through the classification head allows it to specialize its representations for leaf disease patterns — yielding higher accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score on the test set.

ii. Advantages & Limitations

Approach 1: No GPU training; fast iteration; effective with small datasets; SVM is more interpretable. Frozen ImageNet features may miss domain-specific cues for distinguishing subtle disease variants.

Approach 2: Higher accuracy ceiling; learns task-specific features; better generalization with sufficient data. Requires more compute time and data.

iii. Training Efficiency

Approach 1 is dramatically faster — feature extraction is a single forward pass through the frozen network, followed by SVM training on tabular vectors (minutes on CPU). Approach 2 requires many epochs of forward+backward passes, consuming ~5× more time. Both remain far faster than training ResNet50 from scratch, validating the transfer learning strategy.

iv. Strategy by Environment

📱
Resource-Constrained (Mobile/Edge)
Use MobileNet + SVM. Depthwise separable convolutions reduce FLOPs ~9× vs ResNet50, enabling real-time inference on Raspberry Pi and MCU devices.
🖥️
High-Performance (GPU / Cloud)
Use ResNet50 End-to-End. Full fine-tuning leverages GPU acceleration and maximizes classification accuracy.
